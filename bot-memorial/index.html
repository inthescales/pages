Between 2016 and 2022, I made five Twitter bots. Now, four of them are no longer active. This page records their memory. Each was created in a brief fit of obsession, but over the years they exhausted their potential and their time came to an end. I put them to rest as the sun set on the golden era of the art bot, but this was my contribution to that moment.

They are survived by one sibling, Lyre's Dictionary.

My first bot was Required Reading, which I made for Procjam 2016 and later ported to Mastodon. It posted randomly generated textbook titles, themed around different university courses and assigned a price at random. It was a fruitful experiment in recontextualization â€” it worked by finding thematic keywords in texts from Project Gutenberg and clipping surrounding phrases to become titles.

An archive of posts can be found here

My second bot was Gorgar Speaks. Miriam Nadler and I made it together as she shared her love of pinball with me. Gorgar, created in 1979, was the first pinball machine to employ spoken words. Due to technical limitations the machine had a vocabulary of only six words, which were combined in various ways. This constituting a "Gorgar Grammar", it felt appropriate to grant it free rein in a procedural form.

A Gorgar-Grammar generator can be found here

My third bot was Barbarella GIF Bot. It posted a random gif from the 1968 film Barbarella once each day. It was inspired by Hackers Bot on Mastodon, and both share code taken from an earlier work. I was very enthusiastic about the movie at the time, and this was the result.

A collection of hand-cut Barbarella gifs can be found here

My fifth and final bot was Stock Photo Dog. It debuted on November 6, 2022, and I shut it down on November 7, 2022. Sadly some people who adopt a dog aren't really ready to take care of one. It was meant to be an interactive bot that, if you opted-in, would listen to your timeline and respond to any posts that included words like "treat" or "walk" with a photo of an extremely excited dog, intensifying with the number of words. But even after going to the effort of coding it, I got cold feet and didn't want to give myself the burden of maintaining an interactive bot.
